Section 01

Who we are

For the purposes of UK GDPR, Innov8 Workflows is the data controller for personal data collected through this website.

Contact: hello@innov8workflows.com

Section 02

What data we collect

When you use our contact form or interact with our AI chatbot, we may collect:

  • Your name
  • Your business name
  • Your email address
  • A description of your enquiry or requirements
  • Any other information you choose to share in your message

Data collected automatically

When you visit our website, certain technical data may be collected automatically, including:

  • Your IP address
  • Browser type and version

This data is collected via cookies and third-party services. See Section 6 for full details on cookies.

Our AI chatbot is provided by Jotform. When you interact with the chatbot, your conversation data — including any contact details you share — is processed by Jotform on our behalf. Jotform's privacy policy applies to this processing and can be found at jotform.com/privacy.

Section 03

How we use your data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiry or consultation request
  • To provide the services you have requested or are considering
  • To send you a proposal or scope of work where requested
  • To follow up on previous conversations where relevant
  • To improve our website and understand how visitors use it

We do not use your personal data for marketing without your explicit consent, and we do not sell your data to third parties under any circumstances.

Section 04

Legal basis for processing

Under UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis to process your personal data. We rely on the following:

Legitimate interests

When you contact us with a business enquiry, we process your data on the basis of legitimate interests — specifically, our interest in responding to potential clients and conducting our business. We have assessed that this interest is not overridden by your rights and freedoms.

Consent

Where we use non-essential cookies or contact you for marketing purposes, we will ask for your explicit consent first. You can withdraw this consent at any time.

Contractual necessity

If we enter into a contract with you to provide services, we will process your data as necessary to fulfil that contract.

Legal obligation

We may process your data where required to comply with a legal obligation, such as retaining financial records.

All third parties we work with are required to handle your data securely and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

Section 06

Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. We use cookies to make our website function correctly and to understand how it is used.

Essential cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are set in response to actions you take, such as remembering your cookie preferences.

Third-party cookies

Our AI chatbot (provided by Jotform) may set cookies on your device when it loads. These cookies are set by Jotform and are governed by their cookie policy.

Managing cookies

When you first visit our website, you will be shown a cookie consent banner. You can choose to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can also manage cookies through your browser settings at any time — most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking essential cookies may affect how the website functions.

To manage cookies in your browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and Site Permissions
Section 07

How long we keep your data

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by law.

  • Enquiry data — retained for up to 2 years from the date of your last contact, to allow for follow-up on potential projects
  • Client data — retained for up to 7 years after the end of a contract, in line with HMRC requirements for financial records
  • Cookie preference data — retained for up to 12 months, after which your consent will be requested again

When data is no longer needed, we delete it securely.

Section 08

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — you can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — you can ask us to delete your personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to restrict processing — you can ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability — you can ask us to provide your data in a portable format
  • Right to object — you can object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please email us at hello@innov8workflows.com. We will respond within one calendar month.

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) — the UK's data protection regulator — at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

Section 09

Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure.

However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you have reason to believe that your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately at hello@innov8workflows.com.
